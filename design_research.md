# Fantasy Cricket Design Research - Key Findings

## Common Design Patterns from Real Fantasy Sports Platforms

### Color Schemes
1. **Primary Colors**: Green (#10B981, #22C55E) - represents cricket field, sports, action
2. **Secondary Colors**: Dark backgrounds (#1F2937, #111827) with bright accents
3. **Accent Colors**: Orange/Yellow for CTAs and highlights
4. **Card Backgrounds**: White or dark cards with subtle shadows

### Layout Patterns
1. **Hero Section**: 
   - Large bold heading with CTA buttons
   - Match cards displayed prominently below hero
   - Cricket imagery (bat, ball, stadium) as background elements

2. **Match Cards**:
   - Team logos prominently displayed
   - vs. separator between teams
   - Match timing and venue info
   - "Create Team" or "Join Contest" button on each card
   - Green/colored borders or backgrounds

3. **Navigation**:
   - Simple horizontal nav bar
   - Logo on left, menu items center, login/profile on right
   - Sticky navigation

### Typography
- Bold, modern sans-serif fonts (Inter, Poppins, Roboto)
- Large headings (48-72px)
- Clear hierarchy

### Key UI Elements
- Rounded corners on cards (8-16px border-radius)
- Subtle shadows for depth
- Green as primary action color
- Dark text on light backgrounds OR white text on dark backgrounds
- Player avatars in circles
- Score displays with large numbers

### Real-World Examples Analyzed
- Dream11: Green primary, white cards, clean layout
- CricPlay: Dark mode with green accents
- Fantasy Sports Apps: Card-based layouts, prominent CTAs

## Recommended Design Direction
- **Primary Color**: Green (#10B981) - cricket field association
- **Background**: White with light gray sections
- **Cards**: White with subtle shadows
- **Typography**: Inter for clean, modern look
- **Layout**: Card-based, spacious, easy to scan
